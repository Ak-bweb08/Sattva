Mood Disorder

A mood disorder is a mental health condition characterized by persistent emotional disturbances, affecting a person's overall mood and emotional state. This can include depressive episodes, marked by feelings of sadness and hopelessness, or manic/hypomanic episodes, characterized by elevated or irritable moods. Common mood disorders include major depressive disorder, bipolar disorder, and cyclothymic disorder.

Our therapies can improve your self esteem.

Assessment: Understand the nature and severity of the mood disorder.
Therapeutic Alliance: Build a strong relationship for support.
Psychoeducation: Educate about the disorder and reduce stigma.
Goal Setting: Establish clear and achievable treatment goals.
CBT/DBT: Apply cognitive or dialectical behavioral techniques.
Medication Management: Consult with a psychiatrist as needed.
Mindfulness: Incorporate practices for emotional awareness.
IPT: Address interpersonal conflicts and enhance relationships.
Regular Assessment: Continuously monitor progress and adjust.
Relapse Prevention: Develop strategies and involve support networks
